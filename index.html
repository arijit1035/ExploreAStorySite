Turning your explorations into stories

This is the design and development process of ExploreAStory, an app born from a play on the word "exploratory" and the idea that every trip or even local exploration should become a memorable narrative.

During my research I discovered a fundamental need: Modern explorers don't want rigid plans, they crave structured spontaneity.

The User: The Planner's Paradox

Understanding user behavior revealed a clear paradox. People invest time in planning not to create a strict schedule, but to build a flexible menu of options. This section explores who I'm building for and what they truly desire.

How do you prefer to plan?

An overwhelming majority favor flexibility, validating the need for a tool that empowers in-the-moment decisions.

My Design Process

My approach was a blend of structured research and rapid, feedback-driven prototyping. This allowed me to validate core assumptions early and build a product based on proven user needs, not just initial ideas.

1

Secondary Research

I started by diving into existing research to validate the top pain points in the travel and local exploration space. This confirmed that Tool Fragmentation, the Planner's Paradox, and Group Planning Friction were significant, widespread problems.

2

AI-Powered Prototyping (Vibe Coding)

With validated problems in hand, I used AI as a coding partner. I defined the requirements and UI design, then prompted the AI to build a functional prototype. This "vibe coding" approach allowed me to rapidly create a text-based MVP on Google Colab to test the core concept of AI-powered planning.

3

Targeted User Interviews

The MVP prototype workedâ€”it proved the AI approach was viable. I then conducted another round of user interviews, showing testers the prototype and asking: "Given that this works, how would you want to engage with it?" This crucial step is where the key insights about "Structured Spontaneity" came from, directly shaping the final app features.

4

Refined & Deployed Phase 1 App

Based on the interview feedback, I built a refined, usable version of the application with all the core Phase 1 features. This app has been deployed and is currently being tested by an initial group of users to gather real-world usage data and feedback.

5

Next Steps: Validation & Growth

Once the current MVP is validated by these initial users, the plan is to begin marketing efforts to grow the user base. In parallel, I will begin development on Phase 2, which will focus on introducing the collaborative and social features that address the critical 'Group Planning Friction' pain point.

The Solution: ExploreAStory (Phase 1)

ExploreAStory is an intelligent companion that replaces planning stress with joyful discovery. Each feature in this first phase is purpose-built to solve the core problems identified in my research, creating a seamless and powerful experience for the individual explorer.

auto_awesome

Plan with AI

What it is: A smart story builder that takes your preferences and generates a flexible list of personalized suggestions.

Problem Solved: Tackles Information Overload and the Planner's Paradox by curating relevant options, not rigid schedules.

edit_note

Compose Your Story

What it is: A centralized hub combining a map with a flexible list of saved places, replacing your scattered notes and lists.

Problem Solved: Directly attacks the Disorganized Plan and the Dual-System Burden of managing separate local and travel lists.

travel_explore

Explore & Discover

What it is: An in-the-moment discovery engine with real-time suggestions, landmark scanning, and AI-generated audio tours.

Problem Solved: Eliminates the "What Should We Do Now?" Dilemma with context-aware suggestions for spontaneous discovery.
